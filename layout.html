Space, also known as outer space, is the vast, seemingly infinite expanse that exists beyond Earth's atmosphere. It is a near-perfect vacuum, meaning it has very little matter and no air. Space is home to countless celestial objects, including stars, planets, moons, asteroids, comets, and galaxies.

One of the most fascinating aspects of space is its sheer size. The observable universe is estimated to be about 93 billion light-years in diameter. To put this into perspective, a light-year is the distance light travels in one year, which is approximately 9.46 trillion kilometers (5.88 trillion miles). This means that even at the speed of light, it would take billions of years to travel across the universe.
